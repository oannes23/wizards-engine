# Player Guide

A quick reference for playing in a Wizards Engine campaign.

## Logging In

Your GM will send you a magic link URL. Open it in your browser -- the first time, you'll see a join form where you enter your display name and character name. After that, the same link is your permanent login. **Bookmark it.**

## Your Character Sheet

### What You Can Edit

- **Name**, **description**, and **notes** (your personal scratchpad -- inventory, reminders, anything)

### What the GM Manages

- Skills, meters, magic stats, traits, bonds, and magic effects. The GM sets these up using GM actions. If something needs changing, ask your GM.

## Resource Meters

| Meter | Range | How You Get It | How You Spend It |
|-------|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Stress** | 0-9 | Taken from failed actions, magic sacrifice | Healed by Rest (downtime) |
| **Free Time** | 0-20 | Distributed at session start (based on time passed) | Spent on downtime actions (1 FT each) |
| **Plot** | 0-5 | +1 per session (+2 with Additional Contribution) | Spent on proposals for guaranteed successes |
| **Gnosis** | 0-23 | Recovered via Regain Gnosis (downtime) | Spent as magic sacrifice |

## Proposals

Proposals are how you take mechanically significant actions. You submit them, the GM reviews and approves or rejects.

### Session Actions (during active play)

| Action | What It Does |
|--------|-------------|
| **Use Skill** | Roll a skill + optional modifiers. Pick a skill, optionally add a Core Trait (+1d), Role Trait (+1d), and/or Bond (+1d). Spend Plot for guaranteed 6s. |
| **Use Magic** | Freeform magic. Describe your intention and symbolism, choose sacrifices (Gnosis, Stress, etc.), suggest a magic stat. |
| **Charge Magic** | Recharge or boost an existing Magic Effect using the same sacrifice system. |

Session action narratives are optional on submission -- you can add or edit them later while the proposal is pending.

### Downtime Actions (cost 1 Free Time each)

| Action | What It Does |
|--------|-------------|
| **Rest** | Heal Stress (base 3 + modifiers) |
| **Regain Gnosis** | Recover Gnosis (base 3 + lowest magic stat + modifiers) |
| **New Trait** | Replace or fill a trait slot |
| **New Bond** | Replace or fill a bond slot |
| **Work on Project** | Add a narrative entry to a Story |

Downtime actions require a narrative on submission -- describe what your character is doing.

### Modifiers

On session actions and some downtime actions (Rest, Regain Gnosis), you can add up to 3 modifiers:

- **1 Core Trait** -- costs 1 charge
- **1 Role Trait** -- costs 1 charge
- **1 Bond** -- the GM may apply strain (+1 stress to the bond)

### Revising

If the GM rejects a proposal, they'll include a note. You can edit and resubmit -- it stays as the same proposal.

## Direct Actions (No GM Approval)

These resolve immediately when you trigger them:

| Action | Cost | What It Does |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **Find Time** | 3 Plot | Convert 3 Plot into 1 Free Time |
| **Recharge Trait** | 1 FT | Restore a trait to 5 charges (write a brief narrative) |
| **Maintain Bond** | 1 FT | Restore a bond's charges to its effective max (write a brief narrative) |
| **Use Magic Effect** | 1 charge | Activate a charged Magic Effect |
| **Retire Magic Effect** | -- | Deactivate an effect (frees your effect cap) |

## Bonds

Bonds connect your character to NPCs, groups, locations, and other PCs. Each bond:

- Gives **+1 die** when used as a modifier on proposals
- Has **charges** (0-5) representing relationship health
- May be **strained** by the GM on use (+1 stress to the bond)
- Can **degrade** (permanent max reduction) if charges hit 0
- Determines **what you can see** -- events and stories are filtered by bond-graph proximity

## Feed and Stories

### Your Feed

Your personal feed (`#/`) shows events and story entries filtered by your bond-graph visibility. You see activity involving game objects you're connected to.

### Starred Feed

Star game objects you want to follow closely. Your starred feed shows only activity involving those objects.

### Stories

You can read stories you're connected to via bonds. If you can see a story, you can write entries in it -- narrative notes about what your character did or discovered.

## Lost Your Login Link?

Ask your GM to regenerate it from the Player Roster, or refresh it yourself from your Profile page if you're still logged in.
